Model Replication

Unni & Seshaiyer (2019)

Purpose of This Page

This page presents the replication of key simulation results from Mathematical Modeling, Analysis, and Simulation of Tumor Growth with Drug Interventions by Unni & Seshaiyer (2019). While our theoretical modeling is based on Sharma (2022), the simulation graphs in that paper were incomplete regarding equations and parameters. Therefore, we used Unni & Seshaiyer’s fully specified ODE model to replicate meaningful biological simulations.

System of ODEs Used

The model simulates the interaction between: tumor cells, natural killer (NK) cells, dendritic cells, CD8+ T-cells, and drug interventions.

Models of Key Populations

1. Tumor Cells

abnormal cells that divide and grow uncontrollably, forming a mass of tissue known as a tumor

  • : Logistic growth (natural tumor proliferation with carrying capacity).
  • : Loss due to interactions with NK cells, dendritic cells, and T-cells.
  • : Tumor cell death due to chemotherapy, with a nonlinear kill term

2. Natural Killer (NK) Cells

white blood cells that destroy infected cells and cancer cells in your body

  • : Constant source of NK cells.
  • : NK cell recruitment via a Michaelis–Menten-like function.
  • : loss due to competition with tumor cells and dendritic cells
  • : loss due to chemotherapy drug
  • : Natural death of NK cells

3. Dendritic Cells

A special type of immune cell that is found in tissues, such as the skin, and boosts immune responses by showing antigens on its surface to other cells of the immune system

  • : Constant source of dendritic cells.
  • : loss due to competition with Nk and CD8+ cells. rather activates dendritic cells
  • : loss due to chemotherapy drug
  • : Natural death of dendritic cells

4. CD8+ T-Cells

a type of white blood cell that plays a crucial role in the immune system by directly killing infected cells and cancerous cells.

  • : Activated by Dendritic-Tumor interaction.
  • : Competition between CD8+ T cells and tumor cells
  • : describes regulation and suppression of CD8+ T-cell activity when there are high levels of activated CD8+ T-cells without responsiveness to cytokines in the system.
  • : recruitment term proportional to the number of cells killed
  • : Immune activation by immunotherapy drug.
  • : loss due to chemotherapy drug
  • : Natural death of CD8+ T-cells
  • : External T-cell infusion (e.g. TIL therapy).
